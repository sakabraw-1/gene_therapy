The CDKL5 Gene Therapy Journey: A Community-Driven Path

For years, our community held onto the hope of a gene therapy breakthrough. Unfortunately, shifts in research priorities and the discontinuation of programs meant those promises were not realized.

Seeing this gap, the Child's Cure Genetic Foundation‚Äîa non-profit led by parents just like you‚Äîtook the lead in April 2024. We decided that our children could not wait any longer for others to prioritize them.

In the past two years, our team has worked tirelessly and made remarkable progress:

  • We designed and tested multiple gene therapy vectors and selected the one with the strongest results.
  • We completed efficacy studies in mice treated at different ages and held a successful pre-IND meeting with the FDA.
  • We developed the groundbreaking CDKL5 pig model‚Äîthe first of its kind‚Äîwhich shows seizures similar to those seen in patients with CDKL5.
  • This model gives us an unprecedented opportunity to better understand the disease and how this gene therapy may behave in humans, something that has not been possible with mouse models alone.
  • We are now working closely with the University of California San Francisco to plan the first-in-human clinical trial and IND submission.

We are incredibly grateful to the families and clinical partners who made this possible.

Meet Our Clinical Experts

Neurosurgeon

Dr. Gupta is the Chief of Pediatric Neurological Surgery at University of California San Francisco and a pioneer in delivering therapies directly to the brain. He specializes in treating complex neurological conditions in children and has led numerous clinical trials for rare pediatric brain diseases, ensuring the highest level of surgical safety.
